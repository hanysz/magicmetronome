Rest, pauses and silences

A comma in a script will give you a one beat rest. If you want more than a beat, you have two choices. You can use two or more commas in a row. Alternatively, you can use the capital letter "S" followed by the number of beats of silence you want.

If you want less than a full beat of silence, then you'll have to change the tempo (for example, half a beat of silence is the same as a whole beat at twice the tempo).

Examples:

Three ticks, then a one beat rest, then three more ticks, then two beats rest, then three more ticks looks like this:

a a a , a a a ,, a a a

Two ticks then a five beat pause then two more ticks looks like this:

a a S5 a a

Two ticks at 60 beats per minute then half a beat's rest then two more ticks looks like this:

60 a a 120 , 60 a a
