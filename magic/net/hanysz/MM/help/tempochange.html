Gradual tempo changes

To make a section of your script speed up, you need to use an "accelerando block". This consists of a capital letter "A" followed by something in parentheses. For example:

A(60 R5(b a a a) 120)

will give you you five bars of 4/4 time, starting at 60 beats per minute and gradually speeding up to 120 beats per minute.

Things to notice:

  • the letter "A" goes before the opening parenthesis
  • the starting tempo "60" goes after the opening parenthesis
  • the final tempo "120" goes before the closing parenthesis
  • the starting tempo is optional; if you leave it out, the block will start at the same tempo as whatever preceded it;
  • the final tempo is optional, but if you leave it out (or accidentally put it after the closing parenthesis) then the tempo won't change at all;
  • apart from the beginning and end, you're not allowed to use tempo changes during the accelerando block.
If you want something to slow down instead of speed up, you still use an accelerando block; you just make the final tempo slower than the starting tempo. For example:

A(120 R5(b a a a) 60)

will give you you five bars of 4/4 time, starting at 120 beats per minute and gradually slowing down up to 60 beats per minute.
